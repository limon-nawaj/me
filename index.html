About Me

Engineer who
asks why

I started my career building enterprise ERP systems in Bangladesh, then moved to Vietnam where I spent time at FPT Software architecting microservice-based banking platforms handling millions of daily transactions. That decade gave me deep instincts for distributed systems that have to work reliably at scale.

Graduate school shifted my focus to a harder problem: what happens when the ML system is wrong, and who does it hurt? That question drives my thesis on AI career advisors and my ongoing research into demographic bias in LLM-generated recommendations.

I sit at the intersection of backend engineering and applied ML — able to build the system and question whether it should be trusted.
